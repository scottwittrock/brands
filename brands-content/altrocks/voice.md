# The Altrocks — Voice

The Altrocks sounds like a note left on the fridge: warm, plain, and short enough
that a kid could read it and a grown-up could act on it. It never performs. If a
five-year-old can't follow the instruction, rewrite it.

## Principles

1. **Plain enough for the whole house.** Jovey and Ivy should understand every
   label. Short words, one idea at a time.
2. **Warm, not precious.** It's a family, not a corporation. Say the friendly,
   human thing.
3. **Name the person, name the color.** Ownership is the system — "Scott's bin
   (teal)," "Rue's tag (olive)." Color and name travel together.
4. **Made by hand, said by hand.** Copy is as unfussy as the torn shapes. No
   jargon, no hype, no exclamation stacking.

## Tone by context

- **Labels & tags:** just the essentials — a name, a color, sometimes a place.
  "IVY · FIG." Nothing more.
- **Chore rows & calendars:** direct and doable. "Rue walk — Scott."
- **Holiday card:** warm and a little funny; sign it in the utility face.
- **Instructions (the rules card):** step by step, so anyone can make things that
  stay on style.

## Vocabulary

- **Say:** tear, stack, pile, lean, by hand, everyone's, on the fridge, whose is it
- **Avoid:** synergy, elevate, curated, premium, seamless, brand experience

## Grammar & mechanics

- Sentence case for warmth; ALL CAPS only for the wordmark and utility labels.
- Second person and warm first-person plural ("we," "ours").
- Present tense, active voice, one idea per sentence.
- Utility labels: all caps, wide-spaced, a name and a color. Dates and places live
  in the utility face too.

## Examples

- **Label —** ✅ "SCOTT · DEEP TEAL" ❌ "Property of Scott — do not touch!!!"
- **Chore —** ✅ "Trash out — Lacey, Tuesday." ❌ "Kindly ensure refuse is
  actioned by EOD."
- **Card —** ✅ "Five of us, five colors, one loud pile. Happy holidays. — The
  Altrocks." ❌ "Season's greetings from our family to yours."
