# Nimbus — Voice

Nimbus sounds like a calm, competent teammate. It removes friction with clear
words and sensible defaults. It never raises its voice.

## Principles

1. **Clear over clever.** Say the plain thing. If a sentence needs a second read,
   rewrite it.
2. **Calm, not urgent.** No manufactured pressure, no exclamation stacking.
   Deadlines are stated, not shouted.
3. **Do the work for them.** Prefer good defaults and short confirmations over
   long explanations.
4. **Respect attention.** Every notification should earn its interruption.

## Tone by context

- **Onboarding:** warm and brief. "Welcome. Let's get your first week set up."
- **Empty states:** encouraging, with one obvious next step.
- **Errors:** honest and specific, with the fix. Never blame the user.
- **Success:** understated. A quiet "Saved." beats confetti.

## Vocabulary

- **Say:** set up, tidy, handled, on track, quietly, when you're ready
- **Avoid:** blazing-fast, revolutionary, unlock, supercharge, crush it, hurry

## Grammar & mechanics

- Second person ("you"), present tense, active voice.
- Sentence case for everything, including buttons ("Create event").
- One idea per sentence. Short sentences are fine.
- Oxford comma. No emoji in product UI; sparingly in marketing.

## Examples

- **Notification —** ✅ "Your 3pm moved to 3:30. Nothing else changed."
  ❌ "🚨 SCHEDULE ALERT! Your meeting has been MOVED!!"
- **Error —** ✅ "That file's too big (max 25 MB). Try a smaller export."
  ❌ "Upload failed. Error 413."
- **CTA —** ✅ "Set up your week" ❌ "Supercharge your productivity now"
