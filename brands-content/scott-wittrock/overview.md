# Scott Wittrock

**Tagline:** The craft was never the building. It's the *choosing*.

Scott Wittrock is a product manager who has spent about a decade close to AI —
computer vision, visual search, and now language models at Customer.io. The
personal brand spans two properties that share one visual identity and one point
of view: **scottwittrock.com**, the professional home and the record of the
work, and **headforproduct.com**, the publication where the thinking gets
published regularly.

Both wear the same skin — a cool off-white page, ink text, and a single deep
teal doing the accent work — and both carry one idea: *when AI collapses the cost
of building, product craft moves from making to choosing.*

## Personality

Confident about the thinking, modest about the self. Certain about ideas,
generous about people, quiet about credit. A respected colleague thinking out
loud — never a keynote, never a brand talking to a market. The test for anything
that ships: would a smart, senior peer nod, or wince? Write for the nod.

## Two-front architecture

Two sites doing two different jobs, deliberately kept separate so each stays
honest, connected by an identical visual brand.

- **scottwittrock.com** — Personal HQ / the record. Answers "who is this person,
  and should I talk to them?" Leads with the positioning line, the arc of the
  work, the "Building" list, the résumé link, and contact. Links out to the
  writing; doesn't host the essays.
- **headforproduct.com** — The publication. Answers "how does this person
  think?" Leads with essays, the three pillars, subscribe, and the reading
  experience. Doesn't carry the résumé.

Rule of thumb: two publications from the same press. Same press = same tokens,
mark, labels, teal accent, and light/dark behavior. Different masthead = the
wordmark and the emphasis. A visitor moving between the two should feel one
person, one press.

## Positioning guardrails

- **Judgment, not activity.** Foreground decisions and tradeoffs, not task lists.
- **A decade across three AI waves** is the rare, ownable claim — computer vision
  → visual search → LLMs. Most people have one wave.
- **Mobile + developer experience** is the consistent throughline; use it to tie
  disparate work together.
- Keep Customer.io product specifics out of personal writing; frame personal
  projects clearly as personal-time work.
