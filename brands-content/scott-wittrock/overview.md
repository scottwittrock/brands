# Scott Wittrock

**Tagline:** The craft was never the building. It's the *choosing*.

Scott Wittrock is a product manager and builder who has spent about a decade
close to AI — computer vision, visual search, and now language models. He works
at Customer.io, where the team is building an engagement platform for the next
generation of builders — increasingly, agentic ones. The personal brand spans
two properties that share one visual identity and one point of view:
**scottwittrock.com**, the professional home and the record of the work, and
**headforproduct.com**, the publication where the thinking gets published
regularly.

Both wear the same skin — a cool off-white page, ink text, and a single deep
teal doing the accent work — and both carry one idea: *when AI collapses the cost
of building, product craft moves from making to choosing.*

## Personality

Confident about the thinking, modest about the self. Certain about ideas,
generous about people, quiet about credit. A respected colleague thinking out
loud — never a keynote, never a brand talking to a market. The test for anything
that ships: would a smart, senior peer nod, or wince? Write for the nod.

## Two-front architecture

Two sites doing two different jobs, deliberately kept separate so each stays
honest, connected by an identical visual brand. They also carry two facets of
the same person: **Scott Wittrock is the builder; Head for Product is the
leader** — not a manager, but a process and design leader, always a few steps
ahead and thinking a lot.

- **scottwittrock.com** — Personal HQ / the record. The **builder**. Answers
  "who is this person, and should I talk to them?" Leads with the positioning
  line, the arc of the work, the "Building" list, the résumé link, and contact.
  Links out to the writing; doesn't host the essays.
- **headforproduct.com** — The publication. The **leader** — process and design,
  a few steps ahead. Answers "how does this person think?" Leads with essays, the
  three pillars, subscribe, and the reading experience. Doesn't carry the résumé.

Rule of thumb: two publications from the same press. Same press = same tokens,
mark, labels, teal accent, and light/dark behavior. Different masthead = the
wordmark and the emphasis. A visitor moving between the two should feel one
person, one press.

## Positioning guardrails

- **Judgment, not activity.** Foreground decisions and tradeoffs, not task lists.
- **A decade across three AI waves** is the rare, ownable claim — computer vision
  → visual search → LLMs. Most people have one wave.
- **Mobile + developer experience** is the consistent throughline; use it to tie
  disparate work together.
- **It's fine to say I work at Customer.io** — roughly 20–30% of posts might
  mention it. Keep confidential product specifics out, frame personal projects
  clearly as personal-time work, and always delineate which hat is on: the
  personal builder or the day job.
