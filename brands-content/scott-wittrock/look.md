# Scott Wittrock — Look

The look is clean, confident, and professional: a single deep teal doing the
heavy lifting, dark ink text on a cool off-white, and system typography that
gets out of the way. It should read as *credible and current* — a senior
operator's brand, not a startup's launch page.

## Color

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Primary | Deep Teal | `#0b7492` | Primary buttons, links, key emphasis |
| Primary (hover) | Teal Deep | `#085d75` | Hover / pressed states |
| Ink | Ink | `#101828` | Headlines and body text |
| Muted | Slate | `#797c83` | Secondary text, captions |
| Surface | Paper | `#ffffff` | Cards, panels |
| Background | Cool Mist | `#f2f4f7` | Page background |
| Highlight | Notepad | `#fffbe8` | Callouts, highlighted resources |
| Border | Hairline | `#e4e7ec` | Dividers, input borders |

**Rules**

- Teal is the one brand color — one primary action per view. Don't introduce a
  second bright hue to compete with it.
- Text is Ink on a cool off-white, never pure black on pure white.
- The soft **Notepad** cream is for highlighting a resource or callout, used
  sparingly — think a highlighter over one line, not a wall of yellow.

## Typography

- **Headings & body:** the system UI stack (`system-ui`, `-apple-system`,
  `Segoe UI`, `Roboto`, `Helvetica Neue`, sans-serif). Native, fast, and
  unfussy — the words matter more than the typeface.
- **Weight:** headings at 600; body regular. Line-height 1.2 on headings.
- **Scale:** large and confident headlines (h1 ~3.25rem), generous body at
  1.25rem. Give long-form insights room to read.

## Logo & shape

- Primary lockup is the **"Head for Product"** wordmark.
- The mark is a teal rounded square carrying a compass needle — "head for"
  as in *set your heading* — that doubles as an app icon and avatar.
- Corners are softly rounded (12px radius). Nothing sharp, nothing pill-shaped.

## Space & layout

- Max content width ~1100px, centered, with real whitespace around sections.
- Iconography is simple, flat, and single-weight, tinted teal or ink.
- Photography is warm and human (headshots, real settings) — the brand is a
  person, so the person shows up.

## Do / Don't

- **Do** let deep teal and clear hierarchy carry the design.
- **Do** keep type native and legible; prioritize readability of long insights.
- **Don't** stack multiple accent colors, gradients, or heavy drop shadows.
- **Don't** lean on stock-y "innovation" imagery or buzzword badges.
